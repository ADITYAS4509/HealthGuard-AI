Insurance Claims Assistance

General guidance for chronic and long-term health conditions. This page is educational only and does not guarantee claim approval or recommend specific insurance policies.

Insurance eligibility varies by individual policy terms. Always consult your insurer or hospital's TPA desk for authoritative confirmation.

Coverage Types

OPD vs. IPD Coverage

Understanding which type of care applies to your condition determines how your claim can be processed.

OPD
Outpatient Coverage
OPD (Outpatient Department) covers consultations, diagnostic tests, and medications where the patient is seen and sent home without formal admission. Many standard policies have limited or no OPD cover. Check your specific policy document for OPD sub-limits.
Less commonly covered under standard plans
IPD — Recommended
Inpatient Coverage
IPD (Inpatient Department) requires formal hospital admission for at least 24 hours. Most standard health insurance policies are designed primarily for IPD claims. This typically covers room rent, ICU charges, surgeon fees, medicines during admission, and post-hospitalization follow-ups (usually up to 60 days after discharge).
Usually required for chronic condition claims
Process

How to File a Claim

Follow these general steps when initiating a health insurance claim for a chronic or major condition. Specific requirements may vary by insurer and hospital.

1
Admission and Intimation
Notify your insurer within 24 hours of emergency admission or 48 hours before planned admission.
2
Cashless Request (If Applicable)
Present your insurance card at the hospital's TPA desk for cashless facility.
3
Claim Documentation
Collect all discharge summaries, investigation reports, and original bills.
4
Verification and Settlement
The insurance company verifies documents and settles the claim directly or via reimbursement.
5
Claim Tracking and Follow-Up
Track your claim status through your insurer's official portal, helpline, or TPA desk. Claims are typically processed within 7 to 30 working days. If your claim is partially rejected or queried, respond promptly with any additional documents requested by the insurer.
Documentation

Required Documents Checklist

Ensure you have these documents ready before filing a claim. Incomplete submissions are the most common cause of claim delays or rejections.

Doctor's Diagnosis Report (with registration number)
All Original Hospital Bills and Receipts
Discharge Summary (signed by treating physician)
Diagnostic Test Reports (blood work, imaging, etc.)
Insurance Policy Copy (provided by you / your insurer)
Completed Claim Form (from your insurer)
Government-Issued Photo ID (patient and claimant)
Pharmacy Bills for Prescribed Medications
